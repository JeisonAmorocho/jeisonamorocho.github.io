About Me

I am a mathematics graduate student at the Universidad Industrial de Santander. My main interests lie in topology, descriptive set theory, and their connections to analysis. I am also deeply interested in functional analysis and differential geometry. I enjoy exploring how abstract mathematical structures, such as Polish spaces and ideals on \( \mathbb{N} \).

Research Interests

  • Topology of compact-T1-Second-countable spaces
  • Ideals on \( \mathbb{N} \) and hereditary families
  • The Baire property and analytic/coanalytic sets

Current Work

Currently, I am studying compact, \(T_1\), and second-countable spaces (CTS) and their representations through hereditary families, spectra, and the strong Choquet property, as well as extensions that preserve Borel sets.

Teaching

  • Mathematical Analysis II (intersemester course) — Instructor, UIS (2024)

Goals

I aim to pursue a Ph.D. in Mathematics and continue doing research abroad, focusing on topology and descriptive set theory, with additional interests in functional analysis and differential geometry.
