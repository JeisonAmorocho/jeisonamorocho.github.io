Academic life and Goals

I am a mathematics graduate student at the Universidad Industrial de Santander. My main interests lie in topology, descriptive set theory, and their connections to analysis. I am also deeply interested in functional analysis and differential geometry. I aim to pursue a Ph.D. in Mathematics and continue doing research abroad, focusing on topology and descriptive set theory, with additional interests in functional analysis and differential geometry.

Research Interests

  • Topology of compact, T₁, and second-countable spaces
  • Ideals on ℕ and hereditary families
  • The Baire property, and analytic/coanalytic sets

Current Work

Currently, I am studying compact, T₁, and second-countable spaces (CTS) and their representations through hereditary families, spectra, and the strong Choquet property, as well as extensions that preserve Borel sets.
